Is Kinesthetic Teaching What Smart Factories Really Need?

Jessica Villalobos, Enrique Coronado, Barbara Bruno, Alessandro Carf√¨, Fulvio Mastrogiovanni

Abstract

Programming by demonstration techniques have been investigated to facilitate and speed up the setup of new robot tasks. Kines-thetic teaching (KT), i.e., teaching by physically guiding a robot in the execution of a motion, has been adopted in industrial scenarios for its ease of use. In the work described here, we analyse and discuss limits and drawbacks of KT and suggest the adoption of a set of autonomous behaviors
