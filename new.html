Responsible Agentic & Physical AI with Humanity in the HEART

I serve as a Senior Researcher for the Human-Evolving AI & Robotics Research Team (HEART) at AIST.

My research begins with empathizing with and understanding human and planetary needs, using these insights to propose sustainable solutions.

I believe the true value of intelligent systems lies in their capacity to enhance human well-being and safeguard the environment.

Biography

Dr. Enrique Coronado holds a B.S. in Mechatronics Engineering (2012) from Autonomous University of San Luis Potosí (UASLP), Mexico, an M.S. in Advanced Robotics (2017) from the EMARO+ program (Italy/France), and a Ph.D. in Mechanical Systems Engineering (2020) from Tokyo University of Agriculture and Technology (TUAT), Japan.

His career spans pivotal roles in Japanese academia, serving as an Assistant Professor at TUAT and a Researcher at National Institute of Advanced Industrial Science and Technology (AIST).

In 2024, he was appointed Senior Researcher at the Human-Evolving AI & Robotics Team (HEART) team at AIST, where he leads initiatives in Responsible Agentic AI and Robotics. During this period, he has fostered international research collaborations with academic institutions in Mexico, Canada, Norway, and Australia, among others. Since 2026, he has also served as a Visiting Researcher at the University of Tokyo, co-supervising undergraduate and graduate students.
