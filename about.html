Biography & Journey

Dr. Enrique Coronado holds a B.S. in Mechatronics Engineering (2012) from UASLP, Mexico, an M.S. in Advanced Robotics (2017) from the EMARO+ program, and a Ph.D. in Mechanical Systems Engineering (2020) from TUAT, Japan.

His career spans pivotal roles in Japanese academia, serving as an Assistant Professor at TUAT (2020–2022) and a Researcher at the National Institute of Advanced Industrial Science and Technology (AIST) (2022–2024).

In 2024, he was appointed Senior Researcher for the Human-Evolving AI & Robotics Team (HEART) at AIST. He leads initiatives in Responsible Agentic AI, fostering international collaborations with institutions in Mexico, Canada, Norway, and Australia.

Since 2026, he has served as a Visiting Researcher at the University of Tokyo. His work blends science and design to co-create solutions that are trusted, ethically aligned, and dedicated to human well-being.
