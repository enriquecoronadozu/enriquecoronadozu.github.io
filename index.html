Responsible Agentic & Physical AI with Humanity in the HEART

I serve as a Senior Researcher on the Human-Evolving AI & Robotics Research Team (HEART) within the Integrated Research Center for Wellbeing at AIST.

My research begins with empathizing with and understanding human and planetary needs, using these insights to propose sustainable solutions.

I believe the true value of intelligent systems not only lies in their ability to act in real-world contexts or learn from interactions, but in their capacity to enhance human well-being and safeguard the environment.

Biography

Dr. Enrique Coronado holds a B.S. in Mechatronics Engineering (2012) from Autonomous University of San Luis Potosí (UASLP), Mexico, an M.S. in Advanced Robotics (2017) from the EMARO+ program (Italy/France), and a Ph.D. in Mechanical Systems Engineering (2020) from Tokyo University of Agriculture and Technology (TUAT), Japan.

His career spans pivotal roles in Japanese academia, serving as an Assistant Professor at TUAT from 2020 to 2022 and a Researcher at National Institute of Advanced Industrial Science and Technology (AIST) from 2022 to 2024.

In 2024, he was appointed Senior Researcher for the Human-Evolving AI & Robotics Team (HEART) at AIST, where he leads initiatives in Responsible Agentic AI and Robotics. During this period, he has fostered international research collaborations with academic institutions in Mexico, Canada, Norway, and Australia, among others. Since 2026, he has also served as a Visiting Researcher at the University of Tokyo, co-supervising undergraduate and graduate students. His primary research focus is on developing responsible agentic and physical AI systems using transdisciplinary approaches and blending science, technology, and design. He aims to co-create solutions that are not only capable of acting in real environments and learning from interaction, but are also socially accepted, trusted, and ethically aligned to enhance human well-being and planetary health.
